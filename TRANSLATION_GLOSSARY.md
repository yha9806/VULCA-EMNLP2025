## Usage Guidelines

1. **Always reference this glossary** when translating content to ensure consistency.
2. **Do not create alternative translations** for terms listed here unless approved.
3. **Add new terms** to this glossary as they are encountered and translated.
4. **Maintain alphabetical order** within each category for easy reference.
5. **Document context** in the "备注" column to clarify usage scenarios.

---

## Notes

- **RPAIT**: Always keep the acronym untranslated. When explaining the framework, spell out the full names of each dimension (Representation, Philosophicality, Aesthetics, Interpretability, Technicality).
- **Proper nouns**: Names of people (Su Shi, Guo Xi, John Ruskin, Kate Crawford, Mama Zola, Elena Petrova) and places should not be translated unless they have established English equivalents.
- **Technical terms**: For AI and computer science terms, use industry-standard English terminology.
- **Cultural terms**: Terms like "griot", "literati painting", and "defamiliarization" have specific cultural/theoretical meanings and should be translated carefully to preserve nuance.
