## Core Concepts (Condensed 50 References)

### 1-10: Defamiliarization (Остранение)

**Viktor Shklovsky, "Art as Technique" (1917)**

**Core Principle**:
> "The purpose of art is to impart the sensation of things as they are perceived and not as they are known. The technique of art is to make objects 'unfamiliar', to make forms difficult, to increase the difficulty and length of perception."

**Application to AI Art**:
Does AI defamiliarize or automat ize? AI learns patterns → reproduces patterns → risks automation (habitual, familiar). True art makes strange. Can AI break its own patterns?

**Key Concepts**:
1. **Ostranenie** (остранение): Making strange, defamiliarization
2. **Automatization**: Habitual perception (art's enemy)
3. **Prolonged perception**: Art slows down seeing
4. **Difficulty**: Art should complicate form, resist easy consumption
5. **Technique as essence**: Art = sum of devices, not content

### 11-20: Device (Прием)

**The Formalist Method**

**Core Principle**:
> "Art is a sum of literary and artistic devices (priemy) that the artist manipulates to craft his work."

**Application to AI Art**:
What are AI's devices? Pattern recognition, latent space interpolation, attention mechanisms. Can we analyze AI art formalist-style—not asking "what does it mean?" but "how does it function?"

**Key Concepts**:
1. **Priem** (прием): Device, technique, mechanism
2. **Fabula vs. Syuzhet**: Story (raw events) vs. plot (how events are arranged)
3. **Motivation**: Why a device is used (realistic, compositional, artistic)
4. **Dominanta**: The dominant device that organizes a work
5. **System of devices**: How techniques interact structurally

**RPAIT Dimensions**: R: 9, P: 10, A: 10, I: 7, T: 7

### 21-30: Literariness & Structural Analysis

**Roman Jakobson's Formulation**

**Core Principle**:
> "Literariness is a quality that makes a verbal message a work of art."
> "Literature is organized violence committed on ordinary speech."

**Application to AI Art**:
What makes AI output "art" vs. "image"? Formalist answer: presence of defamiliarizing devices. AI often produces "ordinary images"—statistically plausible but not defamiliarizing. Need for AI that commits "organized violence" on visual norms.

**Key Concepts**:
1. **Literariness**: Specific quality distinguishing art from non-art
2. **Poetic function**: Language draws attention to itself, not just message
3. **Equivalence principle**: Pattern, repetition, structure as artistic
4. **Deviation**: Art = departure from norm
5. **System over content**: Form, not message, makes art

**RPAIT Dimensions**: R: 8, P: 10, A: 10, I: 7, T: 7

### 31-40: Automatization vs. Defamiliarization

**Formalist Psychology of Perception**

**Core Principle**:
> "If we start to examine the general laws of perception, we see that as perception becomes habitual, it becomes automatic... such habituation explains the principles by which, in ordinary speech, we leave phrases unfinished... Or why we fail to proofread carefully."

**Application to AI Art**:
AI trained on millions of images = learns habitual patterns. Generation = reproduction of habits. But art requires breaking habits. Can AI learn to defamiliarize its own training?

**Key Concepts**:
1. **Automatization**: Perception becomes unconscious, effortless, dead
2. **Algebraization**: Reducing objects to formulas (art's opposite)
3. **Recognition vs. Perception**: Quick categorization vs. prolonged seeing
4. **Enstrangement**: Making familiar strange again
5. **Revitalization**: Art brings dead perceptions back to life

**RPAIT Dimensions**: R: 9, P: 10, A: 10, I: 8, T: 8

### 41-50: Application to Visual Art & AI

**Extending Formalism to Images**

**Principles for Visual Analysis**:
1. Compositional devices (framing, perspective, cropping)
2. Color as device (not just decoration)
3. Texture as defamiliarization technique
4. Scale manipulation
5. Unexpected juxtaposition
6. Visual rhythm and repetition
7. Negative space as active element
8. Abstraction as making strange
9. Hybridity (mixing incompatible styles)
10. Glitch as defamiliarization

**Professor Petrova's AI Art Questions**:
- What are the constituent devices of this AI image?
- Does it defamiliarize or reproduce habitual patterns?
- Is perception prolonged or instant?
- What is the dominanta (organizing principle)?
- How do devices interact structurally?

## Summary

Professor Elena Petrova offers **pure formal analysis**—no moralism (Ruskin), no spiritualism (Su Shi), no communalism (Mama Zola). Only: **How does this function as art?**

**Key Method**: Decompose artwork → identify devices → assess defamiliarization → evaluate structural system

**For AI Art**: Most relevant critic for analyzing **how AI generates**, not why or for whom.
