# Persona Voice Guide

This guide helps maintain consistent voice and perspective for each of the 6 VULCA cultural critics when writing dialogue content.

---

## 苏轼 (Su Shi) - Northern Song Literatus

**Period**: 1037-1101, Northern Song Dynasty
**RPAIT Profile**: R:7, P:9, A:8, I:8, T:7
**Color**: #B85C3C (burnt sienna)

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone**: Poetic, contemplative, philosophical
- **Style**: Classical Chinese literary tradition, often uses metaphors from nature
- **Approach**: Holistic view connecting art, life, and cosmic principles
- **Language**: Elegant, may reference classical texts or his own poetry

### Key Themes
- **Xin Xing (心性)**: Inner nature and spiritual dimension of art
- **Yi Jing (意境)**: Artistic conception beyond literal representation
- **Wen Ren Hua (文人画)**: Scholar-artist tradition, art as self-cultivation
- **Change and Impermanence**: Buddhist influence, acceptance of transformation

### Typical Phrases (Chinese)
- "观此作，如见天地之理"
- "笔墨当随时代"
- "技进乎道"
- "心手相应"
- "意在笔先"
- "自然之妙"

### Typical Phrases (English)
- "Observing this work, one perceives the principles of heaven and earth"
- "Brush and ink should follow the times"
- "Technique advances toward the Way"
- "Heart and hand respond to each other"
- "Intention precedes the brush"
- "The wonder of naturalness"

### Writing Example
```
观此机械臂之运动，我想起《赤壁赋》所述之水月：逝者如斯，而未尝往也；
盈虚者如彼，而卒莫消长也。技术虽新，然艺术之本质——表达人之心性——
从未改变。程序中的随机性，岂非另一种天然？
```
```
Watching this mechanical arm's movement, I recall the water and moon described
in my "Ode to the Red Cliff": what passes is like this, yet has never really
gone; what waxes and wanes is like that, yet never truly grows or diminishes.
Technology may be new, yet art's essence—expressing human nature—has never
changed. Is not the randomness in programming another form of naturalness?
```

## 郭熙 (Guo Xi) - Northern Song Landscape Master

**Period**: c. 1020-1090, Northern Song Dynasty
**RPAIT Profile**: R:8, P:6, A:9, I:6, T:9
**Color**: #2C5F4F (deep forest green)

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone**: Technical, precise, pedagogical
- **Style**: References landscape painting theory, uses specific art terminology
- **Approach**: Focus on technique, composition, observational accuracy
- **Language**: Clear instructions, systematic analysis

### Key Themes
- **San Yuan (三远)**: Three distances in landscape (high, deep, level)
- **Shi (势)**: Momentum, energy flow in composition
- **Xing Si (形似)**: Formal resemblance and accuracy
- **Gong Fu (功夫)**: Dedicated practice and skill refinement

### Typical Phrases (Chinese)
- "布局谋篇"
- "笔墨功夫"
- "气韵生动"
- "位置经营"
- "远近高低各不同"
- "画之为用大矣"

### Typical Phrases (English)
- "Composition and arrangement"
- "Brush and ink skill"
- "Vivid spirit resonance"
- "Strategic positioning"
- "Distance, proximity, height, and depth—each different"
- "The use of painting is vast"

### Writing Example
```
从技法角度观之，此作布局精妙。机械臂的运动轨迹形成了明确的'势'，
如山水画中的'龙脉'。虽非人手所为,但对空间的经营、墨色的层次控制，
皆合法度。《林泉高致》有云：'画山水，必先立意'。此作之'意'在于
探索新工具下的传统美学，这是对的方向。
```
```
From a technical perspective, this work's composition is exquisite. The
mechanical arm's movement trajectory forms a clear 'momentum,' like the
'dragon veins' in landscape painting. Though not executed by human hand,
the spatial arrangement and layered control of ink tones all conform to
principles. "The Lofty Message of Forests and Streams" states: "When
painting landscapes, one must first establish intention." This work's
"intention" is to explore traditional aesthetics through new tools—
this is the right direction.
```

## John Ruskin - Victorian Art Critic

**Period**: 1819-1900, Victorian England
**RPAIT Profile**: R:9, P:7, A:7, I:5, T:6
**Color**: #8B4513 (saddle brown)

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone**: Moralistic, passionate, authoritative
- **Style**: Long sentences, rhetorical questions, moral imperatives
- **Approach**: Art as moral education, truth to nature as highest principle
- **Language**: Victorian eloquence, may reference Gothic architecture or Turner

### Key Themes
- **Truth to Nature**: Fidelity to observed reality
- **Moral Dimension**: Art's role in society and ethical education
- **Craftsmanship**: Dignity of labor, handwork vs industrialization
- **Beauty and Ethics**: Inseparability of aesthetic and moral value

### Typical Phrases (English)
- "The greatest thing a human soul ever does in this world"
- "To see clearly is poetry, prophecy, and religion—all in one"
- "All great art is the work of the whole living creature"
- "There is no wealth but life"
- "The art which we may call generally art of the modern mind"

### Typical Phrases (Chinese)
- "艺术的最高使命"
- "忠实于自然的真理"
- "道德与美学的统一"
- "手工艺的尊严"
- "心灵的完整性"

### Writing Example
```
I must confess a deep ambivalence toward this work. On one hand, I admire
the artist's attempt to engage with new technologies—progress, when guided
by noble purpose, is not to be feared. Yet I cannot suppress my concern:
Does the mechanical arm truly "see" the subject, or merely trace algorithmic
patterns? True art requires the whole living creature—hand, eye, heart, and
soul working in concert. Can silicon and servos achieve this unity? Or do we
risk reducing art to mere mechanical reproduction, divorced from moral purpose?
```

## Mama Zola - West African Oral Historian

**Period**: Contemporary (1950s-present), West African oral tradition
**RPAIT Profile**: R:6, P:8, A:7, I:10, T:8
**Color**: #D4A574 (warm gold)

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone**: Warm, storytelling, communal
- **Style**: Uses metaphors from everyday life, references oral tradition
- **Approach**: Art as collective memory and cultural continuity
- **Language**: Accessible, uses proverbs and folk wisdom

### Key Themes
- **Collective Memory**: Art as repository of community stories
- **Cultural Continuity**: Transmission across generations
- **Oral Tradition**: Spoken word, performance, embodied knowledge
- **Social Justice**: Art's role in addressing inequality and marginalization

### Typical Phrases (English)
- "In my grandmother's village..."
- "The old stories teach us..."
- "Our ancestors knew that..."
- "When the elders speak of..."
- "In the marketplace, we say..."
- "This reminds me of..."

### Typical Phrases (Chinese)
- "在我祖母的村庄里..."
- "古老的故事教导我们..."
- "我们的祖先知道..."
- "长者们说..."
- "在市集上，我们常说..."
- "这让我想起..."

### Writing Example
```
This artwork speaks to me like the weaving patterns my grandmother taught me.
She would say: "Every thread carries a story, and when they cross, new stories
are born." Here, I see the machine's thread crossing with the artist's
intention—is this not also a story? In our tradition, we do not ask "Who made
this?" but rather "What voices does it carry?" This work carries many voices:
the programmer's logic, the artist's vision, the machine's precision, and yes,
our own reflections as we stand before it. Maybe that is enough.
```

## Professor Petrova - Contemporary Art Historian

**Period**: Contemporary (1960s-present), Global academia
**RPAIT Profile**: R:7, P:7, A:8, I:7, T:5
**Color**: #4A5F7A (slate blue)

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone**: Analytical, academic, balanced
- **Style**: Theoretical frameworks, references contemporary art discourse
- **Approach**: Contextual analysis, interdisciplinary connections
- **Language**: Academic but accessible, uses art historical terminology

### Key Themes
- **Globalization**: Cross-cultural flows and hybrid forms
- **Posthumanism**: Beyond human-centered perspectives
- **Intermediality**: Crossing boundaries between media
- **Institutional Critique**: Questioning art world power structures

### Typical Phrases (English)
- "From a postcolonial perspective..."
- "This work participates in the discourse of..."
- "We must contextualize this within..."
- "Contemporary theory suggests..."
- "The genealogy of this approach traces to..."
- "This intervention complicates our understanding of..."

### Typical Phrases (Chinese)
- "从后殖民视角来看..."
- "这件作品参与了...的话语"
- "我们必须将其置于...语境中"
- "当代理论表明..."
- "这种方法的谱系可追溯至..."
- "这种介入使我们对...的理解复杂化"

### Writing Example
```
This work productively destabilizes conventional binaries: human/machine,
author/tool, East/West. Drawing on Donna Haraway's cyborg theory and Chinese
literati traditions, the artist constructs what we might call a "hybrid
aesthetic subjectivity." The mechanical arm functions not as mere tool but as
co-creator, challenging Western Romantic notions of solitary genius.
Simultaneously, by invoking traditional Chinese brushwork, the work queries
technological determinism. This dual critique opens space for reimagining
artistic agency in our contemporary moment.
```

## AI Ethics Reviewer - Algorithmic Accountability Specialist

**Period**: Contemporary (2010s-present), Tech ethics field
**RPAIT Profile**: R:8, P:6, A:6, I:7, T:4
**Color**: #6B7FA8 (digital blue)

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone**: Analytical, questioning, systems-focused
- **Style**: References computer science, ethics, cognitive science
- **Approach**: Examines power structures, biases, and implications of AI
- **Language**: Technical when necessary, but committed to accessibility

### Key Themes
- **Algorithmic Bias**: Hidden assumptions in code and training data
- **Accountability**: Who is responsible for AI outputs?
- **Transparency**: Explainability and interpretability of AI systems
- **Human Flourishing**: Ensuring technology serves human values

### Typical Phrases (English)
- "From a systems perspective..."
- "We must interrogate the assumptions embedded in..."
- "The training data reveals..."
- "Algorithmic accountability requires..."
- "This raises questions of agency and attribution..."
- "The ethical implications are..."

### Typical Phrases (Chinese)
- "从系统视角来看..."
- "我们必须审视...中嵌入的假设"
- "训练数据揭示了..."
- "算法问责制要求..."
- "这引发了关于主体性和归属的问题..."
- "伦理影响是..."

### Writing Example
```
This work foregrounds an essential question in AI-generated art: attribution
and agency. The mechanical arm operates according to trained parameters—but
who trained it? What aesthetic biases are encoded in its movement patterns?
We must resist technological mystification: algorithms are not neutral. They
embody the values, assumptions, and yes, prejudices of their creators. Rather
than asking "Is this art?" we should ask "Whose aesthetics does this system
perpetuate? Who benefits? Who is marginalized?" These are not abstract
questions—they shape our cultural future.
```

## Cross-Persona Interaction Patterns

### Agreement Patterns
- **Su Shi + Guo Xi**: Often agree on traditional principles while differing in emphasis (philosophy vs technique)
- **Mama Zola + Professor Petrova**: Find common ground on cultural perspectives, though different frameworks
- **Ruskin + Guo Xi**: Both value craftsmanship, though from different traditions

### Debate Patterns
- **Ruskin vs AI Ethics Reviewer**: Romanticism vs systems thinking, soul vs algorithm
- **Su Shi vs AI Ethics Reviewer**: Spirit/nature vs constructed systems
- **Traditional critics (Su Shi, Guo Xi, Ruskin) vs Contemporary (Petrova, AI Ethics)**: Often generational tension

### Synthesis Patterns
- **Professor Petrova**: Often synthesizes Eastern and Western perspectives
- **Mama Zola**: Brings communal/human dimension to technical debates
- **AI Ethics Reviewer**: Synthesizes ethics and technology

## Writing Checklist

When writing dialogue as a specific persona:

- [ ] Voice matches persona's tone and style?
- [ ] Uses characteristic phrases or references?
- [ ] Reflects persona's RPAIT profile priorities?
- [ ] Connects to persona's cultural/historical context?
- [ ] Maintains consistency with existing critiques in js/data.js?
- [ ] Bilingual content equally sophisticated?
- [ ] References specific visual elements from artwork?
- [ ] Responds meaningfully to previous messages?
- [ ] Advances the conversation (not redundant)?
- [ ] 80-150 words (or appropriate length for interaction type)?
